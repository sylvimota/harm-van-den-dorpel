Mutant Garden, Software, 2020

Inspired by the still developing AI art movement, “Mutant Garden” (2020) is an ongoing series started in 2019 by Harm van den Dorpel which uses an algorithm called ‘Cartesian genetic programming.’ In this work, clicking one rectangular ‘mutant’ on the creator’s interactive website allows users to generate art as it causes its sibling to be replaced with newly mutated offspring. Each new innovation in this lineage aimed to improve or declare its predecessor obsolete, yet would simultaneously stand on its shoulders.

As many people tend to equate artificial intelligence with neural networks, van den Dorpel uses this project to reflect on the evolution of computation and our relationship with machine learning systems. The artist’s work calls into question the role of AI within the art world and if its ability to mimic human intelligence generates authentic art. Chronologically, this site would be considered the fifth installment of the artist’s inquiry into the history of computation.

Exhibitions “Modular Mind” (2022) is a collaged example of the artwork produced which was shown at Galerie Noah Klink in August 2022 along with other works from the series. His other works of this series have been featured in exhibitions at: Lehmann + Silva in Porto (May 2019), Takuro Someya Contemporary Art in Tokyo (July 2022), Upstream Gallery Amsterdam (March 2021), Nahmad Contemporary in New York City (June 2022), and Galerie Noah Klink (August 2022) in Berlin.
